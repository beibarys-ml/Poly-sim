1. what is the topic of the text?
The text examines the development of scientific institutions in Europe from the medieval 
to the early modern period, with a particular focus on the exclusion of women. 
It analyzes how access to knowledge, education, and participation in scientific communities 
was shaped by institutional structures. The author questions why women were excluded from 
academies and scientific practice despite their demonstrated ability to learn, teach, 
and contribute to knowledge production.

2.2 What is the question, the author wants to answer? What is she questioning?
The author asks why women were largely absent from the development of modern science 
despite evidence of their intellectual and scientific abilities. She questions whether 
this absence can be explained by natural differences, or whether it is instead 
the result of historically formed institutional structures that restricted women’s 
access to education and scientific communities.
